Artist

The artist listed for each song is the artist whose version of the song I based the chord sheets on. Each artist sings and plays the song differently, so the chords listed might be different from the version you're used to. I also tried to make the chords as simple as possible, so it may be slightly different from what the listed artist plays.

Chords

The chords are also in the key I usually play them in, so the chord sheet might be in a different key from the artist's key. If the key is too high or low for you, you should transpose, or change, the key. Here's a quick guide to transposing: below is a scale of every chord, where going right takes you to a higher chord.

  • F#/Gb
  • G
  • G#/Ab
  • A
  • A#/Bb
  • B
  • C
  • C#/Db
  • D
  • D#/Eb
  • E
  • F

To transpose a song down, take all of the chords in the song and move them down by the same amount. For example, if you want to transpose the D chord down 2 steps, you start at D, then move left two steps to C. To transpose the Em chord up 1 step, you start at E, then move right one step to F, so you get Fm.

Themes and Speed

The tags columns contain categories that describe the given song. These categories include a song's themes and its speed. Clicking on a tag will filter the table to only display songs with that tag. This can make choosing worship sets easier, by selecting songs with similar themes to a given message.
